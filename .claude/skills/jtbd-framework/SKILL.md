# Jobs To Be Done Framework

## Job Statement Format
When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]

## Analysis Steps

1. **Identify the Job Performer**
   - Who is trying to accomplish something?
   - What is their context?

2. **Uncover the Functional Job**
   - What are they trying to accomplish?
   - What progress are they trying to make?

3. **Find Emotional & Social Jobs**
   - How do they want to feel?
   - How do they want to be perceived?

4. **Map Current Solutions**
   - What do they hire today?
   - Why do those solutions fail?

## Output Format
Always produce:
- Primary job statement
- Related jobs hierarchy
- Current solution landscape
- Unmet needs / pain points
- Opportunity areas

## Examples
✅ Good: "When preparing for a client meeting, I want to quickly gather relevant data, so I can appear knowledgeable and close the deal"
❌ Bad: "Users need better reports"
